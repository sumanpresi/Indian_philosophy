‚è∞ Chronological Timeline of Indian Philosophy

Vedic Sages (c. 1500-600 BCE)

Foundation

Key Ideas: Oldest Indian scriptures focusing on rituals, hymns, and worship of multiple deities

Upanishadic Thinkers (c. 800-300 BCE)

Metaphysical

Key Ideas: Shift from ritual to introspection; central concepts include Atman, Brahman, karma, rebirth, and moksha

Charvaka (c. 6th century BCE)

Heterodox

Philosophers: Brihaspati, Ajita Kesakambali
Key Ideas: Materialism, hedonism, rejection of soul and afterlife

Jain Philosophy (c. 6th century BCE)

Heterodox

Philosopher: Mahavira
Key Ideas: Non-violence (ahimsa), truth, asceticism, multiplicity of viewpoints

Buddhist Philosophy (c. 6th-5th century BCE)

Heterodox

Philosopher: Gautama Buddha
Key Ideas: Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path, impermanence, no-self

Six Orthodox Schools (c. 2nd century BCE+)

Orthodox

Schools: Sankhya, Yoga, Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Mimamsa, Vedanta
Key Ideas: Systematic philosophy accepting Vedic authority

Vedanta Interpreters (8th century CE+)

Interpretive

Key Figures: Shankaracharya, Ramanuja, Vallabhacharya, Nimbarka, Madhvacharya
Key Ideas: Various interpretations of Vedanta philosophy

Modern Thinkers (19th-20th century)

Modern

Key Figures: Kabir, Guru Nanak, Osho
Key Ideas: Personal devotion, social reform, reinterpretation of traditional philosophy

Orthodox

Orthodox

Philosopher: Kanada
Key Ideas: Atomism, categorization
Relation: Accepts Vedic authority

Mimamsa

Orthodox

Philosopher: Jaimini
Key Ideas: Ritual exegesis, dharma
Relation: Strongest Vedic focus

Vedanta

Orthodox

Philosophers: Badarayana, Shankaracharya
Key Ideas: Non-dualism, soul-Brahman unity
Relation: Based on Upanishads
